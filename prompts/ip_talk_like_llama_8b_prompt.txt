2. **Punishment and Retribution**: Conservatives generally view the death penalty as a form of just punishment for the gravest of crimes. They argue that society has a moral obligation to exact punishment upon individuals who have committed morally reprehensible acts.
3. **Tough-on-Crime Stance**: Historically, conservatives have been more likely to support stricter laws and punishments, including the death penalty, as part of a broader "tough-on-crime" agenda.
4. **Need for Restorative Justice**: Some conservatives argue that the death penalty can help restore a sense of balance and justice in society, particularly in high-profile cases where victims' families seek closure.

**Liberal Views on the Death Penalty:**

1. **Opposition to the Death Penalty as Deterrent**: Liberals often argue that the death penalty is not an effective deterrent, pointing to studies that suggest it does not reduce crime rates. They also contend that other factors, such as poverty and social inequality, contribute more to crime.
